I was fascinated by the idea of creating something out of nothing, and I wanted to be a part of it. I wanted to create something that can help people, something that can make their lives easier.

Journey and Evolution

I remember back in college, the Fundamentals of Programming subject, that was the time Java was introduced to me by my professor. I am confused most of the time because it was my first time to code. I was struggling to understand the concepts, but I was determined to learn. I spent countless hours studying.

The good thing about that subject is my professor gave a anonymous question board its called Piazza to encourage students to ask questions without fear of being judged. I was the most active user in that board! I asked questions, answered questions, and learned from my classmates. It was a great experience that helped me to understand the concepts better.

Then leetcode comes... our professor gave us a easy to medium leetcode problem for our finals exam. I'm not gonna lie, I was nervous at first because I don't know how to solve it. I searched a lot of resources online, watched videos, read articles, and practiced a lot. I was able to solve the problem, and it was a great feeling of accomplishment.

After that, I started to explore more about programming, I learned about web development, mobile development, databases, technologies, software architectures, information security, networks, infrastructure, and many more. I was fascinated by the idea of creating something that can help people, something that can make their lives easier (and also profit XD). I started to build my own projects, and I was able to apply what I learned in college.

Fast forward today, I am currently a Associate Software Engineer at Amihan Global Strategies where I'm developing APIs using Expressjs and Typescript. Upgrading some legacy Angular codebase and doing things.

In the future, I want to deep dive into Blockchain technology and enterprise systems.
